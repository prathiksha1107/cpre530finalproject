DOS Attack

What is a Denial Of Service Attack?

DOS attack in the Internet Layer can be well understood with the help of a cashier in a super market:

Consider a single cashier at a small grocery store, where the checkout process is quick. Now imagine a group of pranksters decide to disrupt the store's operations. They each grab a shopping cart and join the checkout line, pretending to be regular customers. However, when they reach the cashier, instead of making a purchase, they simply ask pointless questions or feign indecision, stalling the line. Meanwhile, actual customers are stuck waiting, growing frustrated, and eventually leaving without buying anything.

In this scenario:

  • The cashier Represents the target server or network.
  • The The pranksters are akin to a network of compromised machines (botnet) in a DoS attack.
  • Each Genuine customers leaving the store Reflect real users being unable to access the server's resources due to the service being unavailable.

Just like the pranksters' actions prevent real customers from completing their transactions, a DoS attack floods a server with so many requests that it cannot handle legitimate traffic.
