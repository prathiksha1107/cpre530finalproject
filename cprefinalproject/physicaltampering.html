Physical Tampering Attack

What is a Physical Tampering Attack?

Physical tampering attack in the Physical Layer can be well understood with the the analogy of a card castle.

Imagine a child building a castle out of playing cards. Each card is carefully placed, relying on the stability and position of the others. Now, picture another child who sneaks up and pulls out a card from the bottom of the castle. This small action causes the entire card castle to collapse.

In this scenario:

  • The card castle Represents a computer system or network.
  • The careful placement of cards symbolizes the critical components and configurations of the system.
  • The child pulling out a card is akin to an attacker physically tampering with a part of the system.
  • The collapse of the castle represents the system malfunctioning or becoming compromised due to the tampering.

This simple scenario mirrors physical tampering in cybersecurity, where a small, deliberate change can disrupt or damage an entire system.
