TCP Session Hicjacking Attack

What is a TCP Session Hijacking Attack?

TCP Session Hijacking attack in the Transport Layer can be well understood with the the analogy of a movie theatre ticket.

Imagine you're at a concert with a ticket that guarantees you a specific seat. You go to the concession stand, and when you return, someone else is in your seat. They've somehow created a fake ticket with your seat number on it. The usher, who didn't see the switch happen, believes the interloper belongs in your seat because they have a ticket that looks legitimate. Meanwhile, you're left without a place to sit, and the intruder enjoys the show in your stead.

In this scenario:

  • The concert seat Represents the established session between a client (you) and a server (the event).
  • The ticket symbolizes the session token or identifier that validates the client's access to the server.
  • The concession stand trip is akin to a moment of vulnerability when the session can be compromised.
  • The fake ticket holder is the attacker who hijacks your session.

This mirrors TCP Session Hijacking, where an attacker takes over a web session by stealing or forging the session token, and the server, like the usher, cannot distinguish between the legitimate user and the attacker. The attacker then continues the session, possibly gaining access to sensitive information or privileges intended for the legitimate user.
